Working Demonstration — Not a Live Business System

Breakthrough

A recurring back-office reporting service that turns a small business's messy spreadsheets into a clean, accurate, decision-ready monthly report — automatically, every month.

01The Problem

Small service businesses run on spreadsheets — job logs, invoice exports, vendor purchase records, time sheets. The data isn't missing. It's fragmented, inconsistent, and someone has to manually rebuild the same report every single month, by hand, from scratch.

Fragmented

Six or more separate files, each maintained differently, none of them talking to each other.

Inconsistent

The same vendor, customer, or category entered a different way every time.

Repetitive

The exact same manual reconciliation, redone from zero every month.

02The Demonstration Scenario

Ferrous Home Services is a fictional single-location residential plumbing & HVAC contractor with 4–6 technicians, roughly 70–80 jobs a month. Each month, the office manager currently has to open and cross-reference six files by hand:

Job Log

Every job — customer, service type, technician, dates, status.

Invoice Export

Every invoice issued, sometimes missing or mismatched job references.

Vendor Purchase Export

Parts and materials costs, often not tied back to a job.

Technician Time Log

Hours per technician, per job, per day.

Customer List

Customer names and contact info — with real-world near-duplicates.

Payment Status File

Payments received, occasionally applied to the wrong invoice.

03The Repeatable Workflow

The same six-step process runs every month, on any new set of files, without being rebuilt.

  1. 1
    Intake & ValidationEvery file is checked for required columns, valid dates, and valid amounts. Bad rows are rejected with a logged reason — never silently dropped.
  2. 2
    StandardizationService categories and job statuses are mapped to a fixed list. Anything that doesn't map is flagged, never guessed.
  3. 3
    CanonicalizationCustomer, vendor, and technician name variants are matched to one identity — conservatively. Ambiguous matches are flagged for a human, never silently merged.
  4. 4
    ReconciliationJobs matched to invoices, invoices to payments, purchases to jobs — across every file, automatically.
  5. 5
    Business RulesRevenue, cost, margin, overdue status, and data-quality flags are calculated using documented, auditable definitions.
  6. 6
    ReportingFour reports come out the other end automatically — every time, from any new month's files.

04The Reports Produced

Owner-Facing

Executive Monthly Summary

Revenue, payments collected, outstanding receivables, costs, margin, overdue jobs, and month-over-month comparisons — one page.

Operational

Detailed Operations Report

Every job, with its invoice status, payment status, allocated cost, profitability estimate, and any exception flags.

Review Queue

Exception Report

Every duplicate, unmatched record, unknown category, misapplied payment, and outlier — flagged for a human to confirm.

Audit Trail

Data Quality Report

Every source file, reconciled — raw rows in equal accepted plus rejected out, every time.

06System Boundaries — What This Does Not Claim

Please read before drawing conclusions from this demo

  • This system does not guarantee legal, tax, or financial-statement compliance.
  • It does not replace a CPA, bookkeeper, or attorney.
  • It does not eliminate all errors — it flags what it can't confidently resolve, for a human to review.
  • It has not yet been validated against a real business's actual data or workflow.
  • This deployment is a demonstration only — it is not connected to any real business system.

07A Note on the Data
